The major differences between the three references are: 
1. Task5.e (i.e., the english version from Hansard) has no punctuation in the sentences and the translation is far more literal than stiff as seen in the other two versions.
2. There is some nonsense translation in both Google's and Watson's translation (e.g., in sentence 18: "We'm back there, and..." and "This is the style liberal.", etc.).

Using all 3 models would, in our opinion, improve the model's bleu score since it increases the probability of a unigram/bigram/trigram appearing in at least one of the references. However, in terms of translation quality, because both Google and Waton's translation has not-so-good translations in themselves, it may not be the best choice as references if we want to judge our model's translation quality as literal sentences.

The size of traning sample for the alignment model does have an effect on the final bleu score across unigram, bigram and trigram bleu scores; The bigger the training set's size is, the better the bleu scores will be in general. (There are individual cases where having a bigger training sample will cause confusion in the alignment model.)
